Detecting Asymptomatic Circulating Cerebral Emboli

Asymptomatic circulating cerebral emboli can be detected by transcranial Doppler ultrasound.

In most works, Fourier transform-based (FT) spectral analysis has been used. The FT is not ideally suited to analysis of short-duration embolic signals due to an inherent trade-off between temporal and frequency resolution. An alternative approach that might be expected to describe embolic signals well is the wavelet transform.
